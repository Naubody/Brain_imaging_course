# Brain imaging 2026

**Course coordinator:** [Matthias Nau ](https://matthiasnau.com/), **Contact:** m.nau[at]vu.nl

This repository contains the course materials for the course **"Brain Imaging"** (P_MBRIMAG) for psychology master's students at the **[Vrije Universiteit Amsterdam](https://research.vu.nl/en/courses/programming-for-psychologists-5/)**. In this 8-week course, you will learn the basics of Functional Magnetic Resonance Imaging (fMRI), a methodological corner stone of cognitive neuroscience that continues to revolutionise how we study and think about the human brain.

In this course, you will learn what fMRI can (and cannot) do, what it measures, and what research questions it address. You will learn the essentials of fMRI, from MR physics to the neurophysiological underpinnings, how to design an experiment and appropriate analysis, and how to make robust statistical inferences and interpretations. Most importantly, you will get an idea of the awesome possibilities of fMRI, but also its caveats and pitfalls, and build a solid foundation for further learning.

Perussal reading
We will be reading relevant background literature on Perusall and then discuss some of the papers in dedicated journal clubs. The papers will be reviews and/or primary research articles surrounding fMRI and its applications. The relevant papers include the following.

* Finn, E.S., Poldrack, R.A. & Shine, J.M. Functional neuroimaging as a catalyst for integrated neuroscience. Nature 623, 263–273 (2023). https://doi.org/10.1038/s41586-023-06670-9

* Genon, S., Eickhoff, S.B. & Kharabian, S. Linking interindividual variability in brain structure to behaviour. Nat Rev Neurosci 23, 307–318 (2022). https://doi.org/10.1038/s41583-022-00584-7

* Marek, S., Tervo-Clemmens, B., Calabro, F.J. et al. Reproducible brain-wide association studies require thousands of individuals. Nature 603, 654–660 (2022). https://doi.org/10.1038/s41586-022-04492-9

* Naselaris Y., Allen, E., Kay K., Extensive sampling for complete models of individual brains
Curr. Opin. Behav. Sci., 40 (2021), pp. 45-51, https://doi.org/10.1016/j.cobeha.2020.12.008

* Tang, J., LeBel, A., Jain, S. et al. Semantic reconstruction of continuous language from non-invasive brain recordings. Nat Neurosci 26, 858–866 (2023). https://doi.org/10.1038/s41593-023-01304-9

### ** Your Perussal assessment will determine 10% of your final grade.**

Quizzes
Every Module has an associated Quiz that you will do after the lecture. It will take about 5 minutes. Quizzes will become available after the lecture and will be due 3 working days later. If you miss the deadline, you will receive no points for the quiz. Quizzes cannot be re-done, you will have one attempt per quiz.

### ** The quizzes will determine 25% of your final grade. **

Exam
At the end of the course, you will write an exam with multiple-choice and multiple-answer questions. In addition, there will be one open question for which you will write a short essay. Everything discussed in our lectures and practicals will be relevant for the exam. Note that we will not ask you to reproduce equations, will not quiz you on linear algebra, and I will not ask detailed statistical questions. Note that for multiple-answer questions, any possible number of answers might be correct, including one or all.

### ** The exam will determine 65% of your final grade. **
